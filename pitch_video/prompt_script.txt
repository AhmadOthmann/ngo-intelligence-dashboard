# Impact Atlas Pitch Video Script

## Scene 1 – Another Busy Morning

**Visual**

Early morning.

The screen splits into two homes in Europe.

On the left is **Emma**, working remotely for an education NGO supporting communities in East Africa.

On the right is **David**, working remotely for an international animal welfare NGO with projects across Africa.

Coffee mugs.

Laptops opening.

Inboxes overflowing.

Dozens of browser tabs.

Emails arriving.

News alerts.

Funding newsletters.

WhatsApp messages.

Google Alerts.

RSS feeds.

Everything at once.

---

**Narrator**

Every morning starts the same way.

Hundreds of articles.

Dozens of newsletters.

Funding opportunities.

Reports from the field.

Messages from international partners.

For NGOs, finding the right information has become almost a full-time job.

And when that information arrives in different languages...

...valuable opportunities are often missed.

---

## Scene 2 – A Better Way

Both Emma and David close their crowded browsers.

Instead, they open **Impact Atlas**.

The clean white interface appears.

The navigation shows:

Signal Inbox

Saved Signals

Peer Chat

Profile

---

**Narrator**

Impact Atlas was designed around one simple idea.

Instead of asking NGOs to search for information...

AI delivers the information that matters to them.

The right signals.

To the right organization.

In the right language.

At the right time.

---

## Scene 3 – Understanding Every NGO

Emma signs in.

Her organization focuses on education, girls' empowerment and community health.

David's organization focuses on wildlife protection, rabies prevention and animal welfare.

The camera briefly shows the Profile page.

Different missions.

Different regions.

Different funding interests.

Different keywords.

---

**Narrator**

Every NGO is different.

So before AI can recommend anything...

it first learns what matters.

Mission.

Regions.

Funding interests.

Topics.

Preferred language.

Even the organization's own website can help AI better understand what information is truly relevant.

---

## Scene 4 – Signal Inbox

Emma opens her Signal Inbox.

Only a handful of carefully selected signals appear.

One funding opportunity.

A report about malaria affecting school attendance.

A regional education policy update.

David opens his inbox.

His signals are completely different.

An article about wildlife trafficking.

A report on rabies vaccination.

A new international animal welfare funding call.

---

**Narrator**

Neither of them receives the same inbox.

Because neither organization needs the same information.

Instead of information overload...

Impact Atlas continuously filters thousands of public signals...

and delivers only what matches each organization's profile.

---

## Scene 5 – Looking Deeper

Emma expands a funding opportunity.

More details appear.

Deadline.

Funding amount.

Eligibility.

Can this NGO apply?

Peer activity.

Original source.

---

**Narrator**

The inbox stays clean.

Detailed information only appears when it's actually needed.

Everything important is available...

without overwhelming the user.

---

## Scene 6 – Human Decisions

Emma decides the opportunity is valuable.

She clicks **Save**.

A small window appears.

High Importance.

Medium Importance.

Low Importance.

She chooses **High Importance**.

---

**Narrator**

AI recommends.

Humans decide.

Impact Atlas never assumes what is important.

Only the NGO decides what deserves attention.

---

## Scene 7 – Building Knowledge

Emma opens Saved Signals.

Her funding opportunity is already organized.

She expands it.

Reads the complete summary.

Reviews the eligibility.

Changes the status to "Reviewing."

Adds a short note.

---

David opens his own Saved Signals.

His workspace looks completely different.

Wildlife reports.

Funding calls.

Field intelligence.

---

**Narrator**

Saved Signals becomes the NGO's working space.

Not another bookmark folder.

Not another email archive.

A structured knowledge base that grows over time.

---

## Scene 8 – Across Languages

Emma has one question about the funding opportunity.

Instead of searching for an email...

she opens **Peer Chat**.

She contacts another organization already working in the region.

She writes naturally in German.

The recipient instantly receives the message in French.

A reply arrives moments later.

Emma reads it in German.

She never notices the translation happening.

---

**Narrator**

International collaboration should never depend on speaking the same language.

Impact Atlas translates conversations automatically...

allowing organizations to focus on solving problems instead of translating messages.

---

## Scene 9 – Learning Over Time

As both users continue working...

they save articles.

Ignore irrelevant ones.

Review funding opportunities.

Update their profiles.

The AI profile quietly evolves.

Recommendations become increasingly relevant.

---

**Narrator**

Every interaction teaches the system.

The more an NGO uses Impact Atlas...

the better it understands what truly matters.

Not just through keywords...

but through real organizational behavior.

---

## Scene 10

The screen zooms out.

Emma and David close their laptops.

Instead of spending hours searching...

they spent their morning making decisions.

The Impact Atlas logo appears.

---

**Narrator**

NGOs shouldn't spend their time searching for information.

They should spend their time creating impact.

Impact Atlas transforms scattered news, funding opportunities, reports and multilingual communication...

into one intelligent, personalized workspace.

The right information.

For the right organization.

In the right language.

At the right time.

**Impact Atlas. Turning information into impact.**
